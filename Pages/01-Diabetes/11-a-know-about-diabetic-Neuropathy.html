What to know about diabetic Neuropathy?
Diabetic neuropathy is a complication of diabetes that results in damage to thenervous system which worsens over time.
Four main types of neuropathies
  • Peripheral symmetric neuropathy: The most common type of neuropathy that affects the feet and hands.
  • Autonomic neuropathy: Occurs in the nerves that control involuntary functions of the body, such as digestion, urination, or heart rate
  • Thoracic and lumbar root, or proximal, neuropathy: This is damage to more focused areas of the body such as legs, chest wall area. Not widely distributed.
  • Mononeuropathies: These damages are specific to any individual nerves
What to look out for peripheral neuropathy?
  1. Are you feeling any numbness or tingling sensation starting in the toesand fingers then continuing up the legs or arms?
  2. Are you feeling any numbness or tingling sensation starting in the toesand fingers then continuing up the legs or arms?
  3. Are you having loss of balance?
What to look out for autonomic neuropathy?
  1. Do you feel nauseated?
  2. Are you experiencing constipation or diarrhea?
  3. Are you experiencing any difficulties speaking or swallowing?
  4. Are you vomiting several hours after eating?
  5. Do you feel light-headed / dizzy when standing up?
  6. Is your heart beating faster than normal?
  7. Are experiencing excessive sweating, even in cool temperatures or whileat rest?
  8. Are you experiencing difficulty emptying the bladder completely whenurinating / having incontinence?
  9. Are you having sudden muscle contractions / weakness?
Diagnosis of Diabetic Neuropathy?
Your doctor will perform a detailed physical examination

Electromyogram (EMG): Records electrical activity in the muscles
Nerve Conduction Velocity test (NCV): Records the speed at which signals pass through the nerves
