WHAT CONTRIBUTES TO ALCOHOL USE DISORDER?

Scientists are still attempting to figure out what causes alcoholism. It appears to combine at least some of the following:

  • Early childhood experiences.
  • Genetics (DNA)
  • Methods to reduce emotional pain

If a person has the following characteristics, he or she is more prone to develop an alcohol use disorder:

  • Traumatised by incidents of physical or sexual abuse.
  • Consume alcohol frequently, in huge quantities, or begin early in life
  • Family history of alcoholism
  • Have undergone a stomach bypass (Roux-en-y) procedure due to weight problems
  • Have challenges with your mental health, such as grieving, anxiety, post-traumatic stress disorder, depression, and eating disorders
