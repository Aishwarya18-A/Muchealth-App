SAMPLE MENU FOR FIBRE IN YOUR DAILY DIET
FIBRE AT BREAKFAST

You can get about 9.2g of fibre with two large slices of toasted whole wheat bread (6.6g of fibre), one sliced banana (1.4g), and a small glass (150ml) of fruit juice (1.2g).

FIBRE AT LUNCH

You can get about 15.7g of fibre with a baked jacket potato with the skin on (4.7g), half a can (or roughly a 200g serving) of baked beans in tomato sauce with reduced sugar and salt (9.8g), and an apple (1.2g).

FIBRE AT DINNER

You can receive about 9.7g of fibre from a mixed vegetable tomato-based curry cooked with onion and spices (6.6g), boiled wholegrain rice (2.7g), and low-fat fruit yoghurt (0.4g). Keep in mind that fruit yoghurts may include a lot of added sugars, so read the label and try to select lower-sugar varieties.

FIBRE FOR A SNACK

30g of nuts, such almonds, can contain as much as 3.8g of fibre. Make careful to select unsalted, sugar-free nuts.

Total: Around 38g of fibre
